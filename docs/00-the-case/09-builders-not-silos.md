# Builders, Not Silos

This playbook does not argue that product managers, engineers, designers, UX practitioners, QA, security, operations, or customer-facing people no longer matter.

It argues the opposite.

Their judgment matters more, but it has to be applied differently.

The default human participant in this operating model is a **builder**: someone who helps turn customer understanding into shipped product value.

Builders still have specialties. A product leader may be strongest at customer context and shaping. A designer may carry deeper taste and interaction judgment. An engineer may understand the architecture and build the harness, environments, and gates. Those differences are useful.

What changes is the fence around the role. A designer making a working experience does not suddenly make design expertise irrelevant or turn every designer into a software engineer. It means the distance between judgment and a concrete product artifact can collapse. The [Gusto Cofounder example](https://youtu.be/5FKBkUCaLa8) is a useful illustration: a designer could ship working product behavior with the support of agents and the rest of the team.

The point is not role cosplay. It is letting capable people carry an outcome further before a handoff becomes necessary.

Builders share responsibility for outcomes and for improving the system that produces them. Product should be able to move a customer problem toward evidence. Design should be able to encode taste into examples, skills, and gates. Engineering should make safe classes of work flow without requiring an engineer to touch every step.

Specialization remains.

Siloed handoffs should not define how the company creates value.
